# Literacy Sorting Mat System - Redesign Document

## Product Vision
A universal, reusable sorting mat system specifically designed for literacy activities (like Brown Bear, Eric Carle books, etc.) with integrated AAC core word support. The mat is designed to be teacher-facing with student response prompts at the student's end.

## Key Improvements from Current Design

### 1. **Orientation & Layout Optimization**
- **Teacher-Facing Design**: Questions and instructional prompts at top (teacher's end)
- **Student-Facing Design**: Response words (YES/NO/I DON'T KNOW/WHOOPS/UH-OH) at bottom (student's end)
- **Maintains Landscape Format**: Works on desks/tables as a shared workspace

### 2. **Literacy-Specific Features**
- **Interchangeable Title Cards**: Blank space at top for story-specific title cards
- **Character/Object Slots**: Designed to hold cut-out characters from literacy packs
- **Story Sequencing Support**: Beginning/Middle/End options built-in
- **Character Analysis Options**: Who/Where/What/When/How categories

### 3. **Enhanced Core Word Integration**
- **Strategic Placement**: 
  - Narrative/questioning words (I THINK, WHAT, WHO, WHERE, WHY, HOW) at teacher's end
  - Student response words (YES, NO, MAYBE, I DON'T KNOW, HELP, MORE) at student's end
  - Action words (PUT, MOVE, SHOW, FIND, LOOK) on sides for easy reach

### 4. **Modular System Design**
The system includes:
1. **Universal Base Mat** - reusable sorting workspace
2. **Title Card Templates** - customizable for any story
3. **Category Cards** - swappable sorting criteria
4. **Character/Object Slots** - designed for laminated cut-outs

## Proposed Mat Variations

### Mat Type 1: Story Sequencing Mat
**Categories**: Beginning | Middle | End
**Teacher Prompts**: WHAT HAPPENED | I THINK | SHOW ME | TELL ME
**Student Responses**: YES | NO | I DON'T KNOW | HELP | MORE | AGAIN

### Mat Type 2: Character Analysis Mat
**Categories**: WHO | WHERE | WHAT (or custom)
**Teacher Prompts**: FIND | SHOW | I THINK | DIFFERENT | SAME
**Student Responses**: YES | NO | HELP | FINISHED | MORE | AGAIN

### Mat Type 3: Yes/No Question Mat
**Categories**: YES | NO (larger zones)
**Title Space**: Customizable question card holder
**Teacher Prompts**: IS IT | DOES IT | CAN IT | I THINK
**Student Responses**: YES | NO | MAYBE | I DON'T KNOW | HELP

### Mat Type 4: Attribute Sorting Mat (Customizable)
**Categories**: Two or three customizable zones
**Title Space**: For attribute cards (color, size, type, etc.)
**Teacher Prompts**: PUT | FIND | SHOW | SAME | DIFFERENT
**Student Responses**: YES | NO | HELP | FINISHED | MORE

## Design Specifications

### Visual Design Elements
- **Color Coding**:
  - Teacher zone (top): Soft blue/teal background
  - Sorting zones (middle): White/cream with colored borders
  - Student zone (bottom): Soft green/yellow background
  - Title card area: Highlighted with dashed border for easy identification

- **Typography**:
  - Core words: Bold, sans-serif, high contrast (black on light background)
  - Size hierarchy: Title area (largest) > Core words (medium) > Instructions (smallest)
  - Dyslexia-friendly font (OpenDyslexic or similar if available, otherwise Arial/Verdana)

### Sizing & Spacing
- **Overall Mat**: 11" x 8.5" (landscape orientation)
- **Title Card Area**: 7" x 1.5" centered at top
- **Sorting Zones**: 
  - 2-way: Each zone ~3.5" wide x 4" tall
  - 3-way: Each zone ~2.3" wide x 4" tall
- **Core Word Buttons**: 0.8" x 0.5" (finger-sized for modeling)
- **Margins**: 0.25" around edges for lamination

### Accessibility Features
- High contrast text/background ratios (WCAG AAA compliant)
- Icon support alongside words where possible
- Tactile markers possible (raised borders for laminated versions)
- Clear visual zones with distinct borders

## AAC Core Vocabulary Strategy

### Teacher Zone (Top) - Prompting & Questioning
**Tier 1 (Always visible)**: I THINK | WHAT | WHO | WHERE | SHOW | FIND
**Tier 2 (Context-dependent)**: WHY | HOW | WHEN | TELL | DIFFERENT | SAME

### Side Zones - Action & Process
**Left Side**: PUT | GO | MOVE | GET | TURN
**Right Side**: LOOK | SEE | STOP | WAIT | AGAIN

### Student Zone (Bottom) - Responses & Expression
**Tier 1 (Always visible)**: YES | NO | HELP | FINISHED | MORE
**Tier 2 (Expressive)**: I DON'T KNOW | MAYBE | UH-OH | WHOOPS | LIKE | DON'T LIKE

## Differentiation Built-In

### Level 1: Errorless Learning
- Pre-place one example in each category
- Teacher models AAC with heavy prompting
- Focus on matching and following directions

### Level 2: Scaffolded Support
- Visual or gestural cues
- "First-then" sequencing support
- Partial AAC modeling (key words only)

### Level 3: Independent Sorting
- Student sorts with minimal support
- Student uses AAC to explain choices
- Teacher asks reasoning questions

### Level 4: Critical Thinking Extension
- "Why" questions requiring justification
- Multi-step reasoning (cause/effect)
- Student creates own categories
- Peer teaching opportunities
