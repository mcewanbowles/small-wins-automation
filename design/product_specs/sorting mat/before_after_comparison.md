# BEFORE & AFTER COMPARISON
## Universal Sorting Mats AAC - Literacy Redesign

---

## OVERVIEW OF CHANGES

### PROBLEM WITH CURRENT DESIGN
Your current sorting mats are excellent general-purpose tools, but they have limitations for literacy-specific activities:

1. **Orientation Issue**: Core words scattered around all edges - no clear teacher/student sides
2. **Fixed Categories**: Categories printed directly on mat (flies/swims/big/small) limit reusability
3. **Generic Purpose**: Not optimized for story-based literacy activities
4. **No Title System**: Nowhere to indicate which book/story is being used
5. **Mixed Core Words**: Student responses mixed with teacher prompts around all edges

### SOLUTION: LITERACY-FOCUSED REDESIGN
The new design addresses these issues while maintaining all the AAC benefits:

1. **Clear Orientation**: Teacher zone (top) and student zone (bottom)
2. **Modular System**: Interchangeable category cards work with ANY book
3. **Literacy-Optimized**: Built-in title card area for story identification
4. **Strategic Vocabulary**: Teacher prompts at top, student responses at bottom
5. **Future-Proof**: Works with your entire literacy pack product line

## DETAILED COMPARISON

### LAYOUT & ORIENTATION

#### BEFORE (Current Design)
```
┌──────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│ PUT DIFFERENT FINISHED AGAIN WAIT...         │  ← Top edge
├──────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│                                              │
│     Flies          Doesn't Fly               │  ← Fixed categories
│   [sorting         [sorting                  │
│    space]           space]                   │
│                                              │
├──────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│ STOP LIKE DON'T LIKE FUNNY UH-OH...         │  ← Bottom edge
└──────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```
**Issues**:
- No clear "teacher side" vs "student side"
- Can't tell which way to orient the mat
- Teacher and student both reach across to point to words
- Fixed categories limit use to one activity type per mat

---

#### AFTER (New Literacy Design)
```
┌──────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│ I THINK | WHAT | WHO | SHOW | FIND | TELL   │  ← TEACHER ZONE
├──────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│  [Place Story Title Card: "Brown Bear..."]   │  ← TITLE CARD AREA
├──────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│  [Beginning]              [Middle]            │  ← CATEGORY CARDS
│                                              │     (swappable!)
│   SORTING SPACE            SORTING SPACE     │
│   for story cards          for story cards   │
│                                              │
├──────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│ YES | NO | HELP | MORE | FINISHED | AGAIN   │  ← STUDENT ZONE
└──────────────────────────────────────────────┘
     ↑ Student sits here
```
**Benefits**:
- ✅ Clear orientation - teacher at top, student at bottom
- ✅ Title card shows which book you're working with
- ✅ Category cards are swappable - unlimited reusability
- ✅ Teacher models language at their end, student responds at their end
- ✅ Same mat works for sequencing, character analysis, attributes, etc.

## CORE VOCABULARY PLACEMENT

### BEFORE (Current Design)

**Top Edge**: PUT | DIFFERENT | FINISHED | AGAIN | WAIT | I THINK | SAME | HELP

**Bottom Edge**: STOP | LIKE | DON'T LIKE | FUNNY | UH-OH | WHOOPS | MORE | YES

**Analysis**:
- ❌ Mixed purposes (teacher prompts + student responses)
- ❌ Student needs to reach to top for "I THINK"
- ❌ Teacher needs to reach to bottom for "YES"
- ❌ No logical grouping by function

---

### AFTER (New Literacy Design)

**TEACHER ZONE (Top)**:
I THINK | WHAT | WHO | SHOW | FIND | TELL

**Purpose**: Questioning and prompting vocabulary
- Teacher points while asking: "WHAT do you see?"
- Teacher models thinking: "I THINK this goes here"
- Natural reach for teacher to model language

**STUDENT ZONE (Bottom)**:
YES | NO | HELP | MORE | FINISHED | AGAIN

**Purpose**: Response and request vocabulary
- Student points to respond: "YES" or "NO"
- Student requests: "HELP" or "MORE"
- Natural reach for student to communicate

**SIDE ZONES (Optional)**:
Left: PUT | MOVE | GET | GO
Right: LOOK | STOP | WAIT | TURN

**Purpose**: Action vocabulary accessible to both

**Analysis**:
- ✅ Logical grouping by communicative function
- ✅ Each person has vocabulary at their end
- ✅ Supports natural back-and-forth conversation
- ✅ Reduces physical reaching/awkwardness

- Sort characters by WHO (people vs animals)

## DIFFERENTIATION LEVELS

### BEFORE: Limited Scaffolding Options
- Errorless: Pre-place examples
- Scaffolded: Verbal cues
- Independent: Student sorts alone

### AFTER: Enhanced Differentiation

**Level 1: Errorless + Heavy AAC Modeling**
- Pre-place one example in each category
- Teacher models: [points to PUT] "PUT brown bear here"
- Student imitates placement
- Teacher models: [points to I THINK] "I THINK this is right"
- Student points to: [YES]

**Level 2: Scaffolded + Guided AAC**
- Teacher asks: [points to WHAT] "WHAT comes next?"
- Provide gestural cue (point to Middle category)
- Student places card
- Teacher: "Do you need [points to HELP]?"
- Student responds

**Level 3: Independent + Self-Directed AAC**
- Student sorts independently
- Teacher asks: [points to TELL] "TELL me about this"
- Student uses AAC to explain
- Student requests: [points to MORE] when wants another card

**Level 4: Critical Thinking + Complex AAC**
- Teacher: [points to WHY] "WHY did you put it there?"
- Student constructs phrase: "I THINK [this] because..."
- Student creates own categories on blank cards
- Peer teaching: Student explains to partner
