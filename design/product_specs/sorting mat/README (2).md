## 🚀 Quick Start

### Setup (Under 2 Minutes!)
1. Print mat and cards on cardstock (110 lb recommended)
2. Laminate with 5-7 mil laminating pouches
3. Cut out category and title cards
4. Place mat landscape on table/desk
5. Insert title card and category cards
6. Ready to use!

### During Activity
```
Teacher Zone (Top)    → Points to prompts: WHAT | WHO | SHOW | I THINK
Title Card Area       → Shows which book: "Brown Bear, Brown Bear"
Sorting Zones         → Students place: Characters or objects
Student Zone (Bottom) → Student responds: YES | NO | HELP | MORE
```

## 🎨 How To Use: Examples

### Example 1: Story Sequencing (Brown Bear)
**Setup:**
- Use: 3-Way Mat
- Title Card: "Brown Bear, Brown Bear"
- Categories: Beginning | Middle | End

**Activity:**
1. Read the book together
2. Teacher asks: [points to WHAT] "What happened first?"
3. Student places Brown Bear card in "Beginning" zone
4. Teacher models: [points to I THINK] "I THINK the red bird is next"
5. Student responds: [points to YES]
6. Continue sequencing all characters

**IEP Goals Addressed:**
- Sequence story events in order
- Use AAC to respond to questions
- Demonstrate comprehension of story

---

### Example 2: Character Analysis (Same Book!)
**Setup:**
- Use: Same 3-Way Mat
- Title Card: Keep "Brown Bear, Brown Bear"
- **Swap Categories to**: Who | Where | What

**Activity:**
1. Sort characters by WHO (people vs animals)
2. Sort by WHERE (sky/ground/water)
3. Sort by WHAT they are (bird/bear/fish/etc.)

**Benefits:**
- Same mat, different learning target
- 30-second category swap
- Multiple exposures to same book
- Different cognitive skills practiced

---

### Example 3: Yes/No Comprehension
**Setup:**
- Use: Yes/No Mat
- Title Card: "Brown Bear, Brown Bear"
- Categories: YES | NO (built-in)

**Questions:**
- "Is the bear blue?" → NO
- "Does the story have a red bird?" → YES
- "Is this a true story?" → NO
- "Did you like the book?" → YES

**AAC Modeling:**
- Teacher: [points to IS IT] "Is it make-believe?"
- Student: [points to YES]
- Teacher: [points to I THINK] "I think so too!"

## 🔧 Differentiation Guide

### Level 1: Errorless Learning
**Setup:**
- Pre-place one example in each category
- Use hand-over-hand guidance
- Heavy AAC modeling by teacher

**Example:**
- Brown Bear already in "Beginning" zone
- Teacher: [points to PUT] "Put red bird next"
- Physical prompt to help student place card
- Teacher: [points to YES] "Yes! Good job!"

---

### Level 2: Scaffolded Support
**Setup:**
- Provide verbal or gestural cues
- Partial physical prompts
- Model first, then student imitates

**Example:**
- Teacher sorts first character while modeling
- Teacher: "Your turn. [points to FIND] Find the next one"
- Student sorts with gestural cues
- Teacher: "Need [points to HELP]?"

---

### Level 3: Independent Practice
**Setup:**
- Student sorts without prompts
- Teacher asks comprehension questions
- Student uses AAC to explain

**Example:**
- Student independently places all cards
- Teacher: [points to TELL] "Tell me about this one"
- Student: [points to I THINK] then places card
- Teacher: "Why?" Student points to category

---

### Level 4: Critical Thinking
**Setup:**
- Ask reasoning questions
- Student creates own categories
- Peer teaching opportunities

**Example:**
- Teacher: [points to WHY] "Why did you put it there?"
- Student constructs response using core words
- Student teaches peer using mat
- Student creates custom category cards

## 💡 Pro Tips

### Storage Solutions
- **Mats**: Store flat in file folder or large envelope
- **Cards**: Small plastic photo boxes or labeled envelopes
- **By Book**: Create "book bags" with title card + relevant category cards
- **By Theme**: Organize by literacy skill (sequencing, character analysis, etc.)

### Laminating Tips
- Use 5 mil for cards (flexible), 7 mil for mats (sturdy)
- Leave 1/8" border when cutting laminated items
- Round corners for safety (corner rounder punch)
- Write on laminated cards with dry-erase markers

### Center Rotation Ideas
- **Monday**: New book introduction + character sort
- **Tuesday**: Same book, story sequence
- **Wednesday**: Different book, same categories (generalization)
- **Thursday**: Review week's books with student choice
- **Friday**: Assessment - independent sorting
