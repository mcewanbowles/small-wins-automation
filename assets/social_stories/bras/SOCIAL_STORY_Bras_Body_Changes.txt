## PAGE 2: MY BODY IS CHANGING

**TEXT:**
```
GROWING UP

When girls get older, their bodies change.
One change is that my chest (breasts) starts to grow.

This is called BREAST DEVELOPMENT.
It is NORMAL and HEALTHY.

Breast development means:
• My body is going through puberty
• This happens to ALL girls
• It means I'm growing up
• My body is working correctly

Breast development usually starts:
• Between ages 8-13
• Some girls start earlier
• Some girls start later
• Everyone is different - this is okay!

WHAT I MIGHT NOTICE:
• Small bumps under my nipples
• Tenderness or soreness
• One side might grow faster (this is normal!)
• Breasts gradually get bigger over time

This process can take 2-4 years!
```

## PAGE 3: WHAT IS A BRA?

**TEXT:**
```
WHAT IS A BRA?

A bra is a piece of clothing that:
• Supports my breasts
• Provides comfort
• Gives me privacy under my clothes
• Some girls wear for sports or activities

TYPES OF BRAS:

For beginners (just starting):
• Training bra (soft, no wires)
• Sports bra (comfortable, stretchy)
• Bralette (soft, no structure)
• Camisole with shelf bra (built-in support)

For later (more development):
• Underwire bra (more support)
• Wireless bra (comfortable support)
• Sports bra (for exercise)

NOT EVERY GIRL NEEDS A BRA RIGHT AWAY!

It depends on:
• How much my breasts have grown
• If I feel comfortable without one
• If activities make me uncomfortable without one
• What feels right for me

There's no rush!
```

## PAGE 6: BRA SIZES & FITTING

**TEXT:**
```
UNDERSTANDING BRA SIZES

FIRST BRAS:
Usually sized by age or simple sizes:
• Training bra (no size needed)
• XS, S, M, L
• 30, 32, 34 (chest measurement)

LATER (when breasts develop more):
Bra sizes have TWO parts:
• BAND SIZE: Number (32, 34, 36) = inches around chest
• CUP SIZE: Letter (A, B, C) = breast size

Example: 34B means:
• 34 inches around chest
• B cup breast size

HOW TO MEASURE:

1. BAND SIZE:
   • Measure around chest (under breasts)
   • Round to nearest even number
   • Example: 33 inches = size 34

2. CUP SIZE:
   • Measure around fullest part of chest
   • Subtract band size
   • Difference = cup size
   • 1 inch = A, 2 inches = B, 3 inches = C

OR: Ask for help measuring at store!
Many stores (Target, JCPenney) will help you measure.

SIGNS BRA FITS WELL:
✓ Comfortable to wear all day
✓ Doesn't ride up in back
✓ Straps don't fall down
✓ Covers everything
✓ Feels supportive but not tight

SIGNS BRA DOESN'T FIT:
✗ Uncomfortable or painful
✗ Rides up or moves around
✗ Leaves red marks
✗ Too tight or too loose
✗ Straps fall down constantly

It might take trying several before I find the right fit!
This is NORMAL!
```

## PAGE 7: PUTTING ON A BRA

**TEXT:**
```
HOW TO PUT ON A BRA

METHOD 1: OVER THE HEAD (Sports bras/Bralettes)
1. Put arms through straps
2. Pull over head
3. Pull down to cover chest
4. Adjust if needed

METHOD 2: HOOK IN FRONT, TURN (Hook bras)
1. Put bra on backwards (hooks in front)
2. Hook the clasp
3. Turn bra around (hooks in back now)
4. Put arms through straps
5. Adjust straps and position

METHOD 3: HOOK IN BACK (Advanced)
1. Put arms through straps
2. Hold bra in place
3. Reach behind and hook clasp
4. Adjust straps and position

Most beginners use Method 1 or 2!
Method 3 takes practice.

ADJUSTING:
• Make sure everything is covered
• Adjust straps (not too tight or loose)
• Make sure bra sits flat against chest
• Should feel comfortable

PRACTICE!
The first few times might be tricky.
It gets easier with practice!
```

## PAGE 8: TAKING CARE OF MY BRAS

**TEXT:**
```
BRA CARE

HOW TO WASH:

BEST WAY:
• Hand wash in sink with gentle soap
• Rinse well
• Lay flat or hang to dry
• DON'T put in dryer (stretches them out)

EASIER WAY:
• Put in lingerie bag or pillowcase
• Wash on gentle cycle
• Hang to dry
• Still don't use dryer!

HOW OFTEN TO WASH:
• Sports bras: After each wear
• Regular bras: After 2-3 wears
• Unless sweaty or dirty: wash sooner

HOW MANY TO OWN:
• Start with: 2-3 bras
• Eventually have: 5-7 bras
• This way there's always a clean one

WHEN TO REPLACE:
• When it's stretched out
• When straps are loose
• When it doesn't fit anymore
• When it's damaged or worn
• Usually every 6-12 months

WHERE TO STORE:
• In dresser drawer
• Laid flat (don't fold cups inside out)
• OR hang on hanger
• Keep in private space (my room)
```

## PAGE 10: COMMON QUESTIONS & CONCERNS

**TEXT:**
```
QUESTIONS GIRLS ASK

Q: What if my breasts are different sizes?
A: This is NORMAL! Most girls have one breast slightly bigger than the other. It usually evens out as you finish developing. Bras will still fit fine.

Q: What if my breasts hurt?
A: Soreness and tenderness are normal during growth. They might hurt before your period too. Wear a supportive bra and let parent know if pain is severe.

Q: What if kids at school notice I'm wearing a bra?
A: Lots of girls your age wear bras! If someone notices, they're probably wearing one too. You don't need to discuss it if you don't want to.

Q: What if I don't want to wear a bra?
A: It's your body and your choice! Talk to parent about options. Some girls wear camisoles or undershirts instead. Find what's comfortable for YOU.

Q: Do I have to wear a bra to school?
A: Check with your parent. Most schools don't require it, but many girls feel more comfortable wearing one. Your choice!

Q: What if my friends are wearing bras but I'm not ready?
A: Everyone develops at different times! There's no rush. Wait until YOU'RE ready, not when friends are ready.

Q: Can boys see my bra through my shirt?
A: Choose bras that match your skin tone or are white/neutral. These are harder to see through light-colored shirts. You can also layer with a camisole.

Q: What should I do if my bra strap keeps falling?
A: Tighten the strap adjusters, try a different style, or get a different size. Straps shouldn't fall down constantly.

Q: What if I feel embarrassed shopping for bras?
A: LOTS of girls feel this way at first! Bring a trusted female adult. Remember: store workers help MANY girls every day. This is their job and they're happy to help!
```

## PAGE 12: PARENT/EDUCATOR GUIDE

**TEXT:**
```
GUIDE FOR PARENTS & EDUCATORS

WHY THIS CONVERSATION MATTERS:

Girls with autism/special needs may struggle with:
• Recognizing signs of breast development
• Understanding the need for a bra
• Sensory sensitivities to bra materials
• Shopping and fitting process
• Privacy rules around changing
• Social aspects of puberty

This guide provides explicit, concrete information!

---

## TEACHING TIPS:

1. **START BEFORE NEEDED**
   - Introduce concept around age 8-9
   - Before actual need arises
   - Normalize bras as regular clothing
   - Point out older girls/women wearing them

2. **ADDRESS SENSORY ISSUES**
   - Try multiple styles (soft, wireless, stretchy)
   - Start with sports bras or bralettes (most comfortable)
   - Let her wear it around the house first
   - Gradually increase wearing time
   - Consider seamless or tagless options
   - Cotton or bamboo fabrics often better than synthetic

3. **MAKE SHOPPING POSITIVE**
   - Go to quiet stores during off-peak hours
   - Order online to try at home (easier, less pressure)
   - Don't force fitting room if overwhelming
   - Can try over shirt first
   - Let her choose styles/colors she likes
   - Keep it low-pressure, supportive

4. **TEACH PRIVATE VS PUBLIC**
   - Use social story repeatedly
   - Practice at home
   - Remind before school/activities
   - Use visual cue card if needed
   - Don't assume she knows these rules!

5. **NORMALIZE DEVELOPMENT**
   - Read books about puberty together
   - Watch age-appropriate videos
   - Explain it's happening to all girls
   - Share your own experience if comfortable
   - Connect with other moms of daughters

---

## SENSORY ACCOMMODATIONS:

If traditional bras are too uncomfortable:

ALTERNATIVES:
• Soft, wireless sports bras
• Bralettes (no structure, very soft)
• Camisoles with built-in shelf bra
• Compression camisoles
• Longer undershirts that provide coverage
• Soft cotton bras without elastic or seams

START GRADUAL:
• Wear 1 hour at a time
• Gradually increase
• Provide breaks
• Don't force all day right away

DESENSITIZATION:
• Let her touch/hold bras
• Wear over shirt first
• Use weighted blanket to simulate pressure
• Practice deep pressure before putting on

---

## FITTING GUIDE FOR PARENTS:

**MEASURING AT HOME:**
1. Use soft measuring tape
2. Measure around chest (under breasts) for band
3. Measure around fullest part for cup
4. Use online sizing chart
5. When in doubt, order multiple sizes

**SIGNS OF GOOD FIT:**
- Band is level all around (not riding up)
- Gore (center) lies flat against chest
- Cups contain everything (no spillage)
- Straps stay up but don't dig in
- Comfortable for hours

**SIGNS OF POOR FIT:**
- Red marks or pain
- Bra rides up in back
- Straps fall down
- Cups wrinkle or gap
- Everything doesn't stay in place

**SIZE CHANGES:**
- Remeasure every 6 months during growth
- Size will change as she develops
- Normal to need new bras regularly during puberty

---

## ADDRESSING RESISTANCE:

**IF SHE REFUSES TO WEAR A BRA:**

Understand why:
• Sensory sensitivity?
• Doesn't understand need?
• Embarrassment?
• Not ready?

STRATEGIES:
1. Don't force (creates negative association)
2. Try different types/materials
3. Start with non-structured options
4. Explain concrete reasons (privacy, comfort)
5. Let her observe peers (girls her age wearing them)
6. Use visual supports showing when/where to wear
7. Gradually build tolerance

**WHEN IT'S NECESSARY:**
- If development is visible through clothing
- For sports/physical activities
- For modesty in public
- For comfort during growth

Balance comfort with social appropriateness.

---

## PRIVACY TEACHING:

**CONCRETE RULES TO TEACH:**

Changing:
• Only in: bedroom (door closed), bathroom (locked), dressing room
• Never in: classroom, hallway, living room, car, public

Adjusting:
• Go to bathroom for adjustments
• Quick strap fix through clothing okay (brief, discrete)
• No reaching inside shirt in public

Discussing:
• Okay with: mom, female family, close friends (privately), doctor
• Not with: boys, in class, loudly in public

**SCHOOL-SPECIFIC:**
- Change in locker room stall if available
- Face locker while changing
- Be quick and discrete
- Keep extra bra in backpack if needed

---

## COMMON PARENT CONCERNS:

**"She's too young!"**
- Development age varies (8-13 is normal range)
- Watch for physical signs, not just age
- Early developer may need support younger

**"She doesn't want to grow up."**
- Validate feelings
- Normalize changes
- Focus on practical aspects, not "growing up"
- Frame as "body doing what it's supposed to"

**"What about locker room changing?"**
- Practice at home
- Visit school locker room when empty
- Request accommodation if needed (bathroom stall)
- Most schools have private changing options

**"She won't keep bra on."**
- Start gradual (1 hour, increase slowly)
- Check fit (might be uncomfortable)
- Try different types
- Use visual schedule for when to wear
- Make it part of routine (like socks)

---

## RED FLAGS - CONSULT DOCTOR IF:

⚠️ Breast development before age 7 (precocious puberty)
⚠️ No development by age 14 (delayed puberty)
⚠️ Severe breast pain
⚠️ Lumps or unusual changes
⚠️ One breast dramatically different from other
⚠️ Discharge from nipples
⚠️ Skin changes (redness, dimpling)

Early or delayed development may be normal, but worth checking!
```
