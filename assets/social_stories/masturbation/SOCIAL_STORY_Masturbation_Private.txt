## PAGE 6: WHAT IF I HAVE THE URGE IN PUBLIC?

**TEXT:**
```
MANAGING URGES IN PUBLIC

Sometimes I might have the urge to touch my private parts when I'm NOT in private.

This is normal!
But I must WAIT.

WHAT TO DO:

1. STOP
   - Do not touch my private parts
   - Keep hands away

2. REDIRECT
   - Think about something else
   - Do a different activity
   - Talk to someone
   - Move to a different room

3. WAIT
   - Wait until I'm home
   - Wait until I'm in my private bedroom or bathroom
   - The urge will pass or I can handle it later in private

4. REMIND MYSELF
   - "This is private"
   - "I can wait"
   - "I'll do this later at home"

IF IT'S VERY HARD TO WAIT:
• Go to bathroom (but just to wait/calm down, NOT to masturbate)
• Wash face with cold water
• Take deep breaths
• Think about homework, games, or other topics

This takes practice!
It's okay if it's hard at first.
I can learn to wait.

## PAGE 11: PARENT/EDUCATOR GUIDE

**TEXT:**
```
GUIDE FOR PARENTS & EDUCATORS

⚠️ WHY THIS TOPIC IS CRITICAL

Students with autism/developmental disabilities often struggle with understanding social boundaries around private behaviors. Without explicit teaching, they may:
- Engage in masturbation in public settings
- Face social rejection or ridicule  
- Encounter legal consequences
- Experience trauma from negative reactions

This social story provides clear, concrete rules to prevent these outcomes.

---

## TEACHING APPROACH

1. **USE CLINICAL LANGUAGE**
   - Say "masturbation" and "private parts"
   - Don't use euphemisms or vague terms
   - Be matter-of-fact, not embarrassed
   - Frame as safety/social rules education

2. **BE EXPLICIT ABOUT LOCATIONS**
   - Students need concrete places, not vague concepts
   - "Bedroom door closed" not "when appropriate"
   - Create visual list of okay/not okay locations
   - Use photos of actual rooms if needed

3. **PRACTICE WITH SCENARIOS**
   - "You're in the living room and have an urge. What do you do?"
   - Role-play the STOP-REDIRECT-WAIT sequence
   - Rehearse what to say if reminded by adult
   - Practice multiple times

4. **CREATE VISUAL SUPPORTS**
   - Laminate "Private vs Public" card for bedroom
   - Visual checklist of private behaviors
   - Traffic light visual for urges
   - Keep visual reminders accessible

5. **TEACH WITHOUT SHAME**
   - NEVER shame the behavior itself
   - Only redirect the LOCATION
   - Emphasize "normal" and "everyone does this"
   - Validate feelings while teaching rules

---

## FOR SCHOOL SETTINGS

**IF STUDENT MASTURBATES AT SCHOOL:**

DO:
✓ Interrupt calmly: "That's private. Hands out of pants."
✓ Redirect to appropriate activity
✓ Document incident privately
✓ Communicate with parents discreetly
✓ Reteach using this social story
✓ Create behavior intervention plan if ongoing

DON'T:
✗ Shame or punish
✗ Announce publicly
✗ Make big scene
✗ Use this as discipline issue
✗ Assume student is being defiant

**PREVENTION STRATEGIES:**
- Keep student engaged in activities
- Provide sensory alternatives (fidgets, stress balls)
- Monitor during transitions
- Provide private bathroom breaks
- Teach replacement behaviors
- Use visual reminders

---

## FOR HOME SETTINGS

**CREATING PRIVACY AT HOME:**

1. **Teach door signals:**
   - Closed door = private, don't enter
   - Open door = okay to enter
   - Knock and wait for "come in"

2. **Establish routines:**
   - Link to other private activities (shower, changing)
   - Acceptable times (before bed, after shower)
   - Make it predictable

3. **Provide appropriate outlet:**
   - Student DOES need private time alone
   - This is healthy and normal
   - Don't restrict all privacy
   - Just teach WHEN and WHERE

4. **Handle discovery calmly:**
   - If you walk in accidentally: "Sorry, I'll give you privacy"
   - Don't shame or make big deal
   - Reinforce door-locking
   - Respect privacy

---

## ADDRESSING COMMON CONCERNS

**"Isn't this teaching them about masturbation?"**
- They likely already know/are doing it
- Teaching locations, not introducing the behavior
- Not teaching is more dangerous (public incidents)
- This is harm reduction, not encouragement

**"What about religion/values?"**
- Frame as safety/social rules, not morality
- Focus on location only
- Respect family values while teaching practical skills
- Goal is preventing trauma and legal issues

**"What if they do it too much?"**
- If it interferes with daily life, consult doctor/therapist
- May indicate need for more activities/engagement
- Could signal anxiety or sensory seeking
- Medical/therapeutic issue, not discipline issue

**"What if they don't understand privacy?"**
- Start with concrete rules, not abstract concepts
- Use visual supports extensively
- Practice with role-play
- May need individualized behavior plan

---

## RED FLAGS - SEEK PROFESSIONAL HELP IF:

⚠️ Student masturbates in public despite repeated teaching
⚠️ Behavior is compulsive/interferes with functioning
⚠️ Student tries to involve others or engage in public
⚠️ Behavior occurs after trauma (could be sign of abuse)
⚠️ Student becomes aggressive when redirected

Contact: Behavior therapist, psychologist, or medical doctor
