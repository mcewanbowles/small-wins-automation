```
When boys get older, their bodies change.
```

## PAGE 4: WHAT TO DO WHEN IT HAPPENS

### TEXT:
```
WHAT TO DO

When I have an erection, I can:

✓ Stay calm - this is normal
✓ Think about something else
✓ Wait - it will go away on its own
✓ Cover with a book, backpack, or jacket if I'm in public
✓ Go to the bathroom if needed
✓ Remember: Everyone goes through this

PRIVATE BEHAVIORS:
Some things are only okay in PRIVATE:
• Touching my penis
• Adjusting my clothes "down there"
• Checking if erection is gone

PRIVATE means:
• In my bedroom with door closed
• In the bathroom with door locked
• NOT at school, NOT in public, NOT around others

If I need to adjust, I go somewhere private.
```

```
WET DREAMS

Another change that happens is wet dreams.

A wet dream is when:
• I have a dream while sleeping
• Fluid (called semen) comes out of my penis

This is NORMAL and HEALTHY!

Wet dreams mean:
• My body is working correctly
• I am going through puberty
• I am growing up

Wet dreams happen to ALL boys.
Some boys have them often.
Some boys have them rarely.
Both are normal!

I cannot control wet dreams.
They happen while I'm asleep.
This is not my fault.
```

## PAGE 8: PARENT/EDUCATOR GUIDE

### TEXT:
```
GUIDE FOR PARENTS & EDUCATORS

TEACHING TIPS:

1. READ TOGETHER:
   - Review this story with your son multiple times
   - Let him ask questions without judgment
   - Normalize the conversation

2. PRIVATE VS PUBLIC:
   - Reinforce what behaviors are private
   - Practice asking "Is this private or public?"
   - Role-play appropriate responses

3. DISCUSSION STARTERS:
   - "What changes have you noticed in your body?"
   - "Do you have any questions about erections or wet dreams?"
   - "Who can you talk to if you're worried or confused?"

4. PRACTICAL SUPPORT:
   - Keep extra underwear in backpack (discreet)
   - Teach laundry skills for independence
   - Have mattress protector on bed (practical, not shameful)

5. LANGUAGE TO USE:
   ✓ "This is normal"
   ✓ "All boys experience this"
   ✓ "Your body is working correctly"
   ✓ "You're growing up"
   
   AVOID:
   ✗ Shaming language
   ✗ "You're too young to know"
   ✗ Avoiding the conversation
   ✗ Acting embarrassed

6. WHEN TO SEEK HELP:
   - If your son seems excessively worried
   - If private behaviors happen in public repeatedly
   - If you need support having conversations
   
   Contact: Pediatrician, therapist, or autism specialist

ADDITIONAL RESOURCES:
- Talk to your son's doctor about puberty education
- Look for puberty classes designed for boys with autism
- Consider books designed for visual learners

Remember: Open, honest, shame-free communication is key!
```
